Suffixes

Suffix Explanation

As a kit gets older, their name suffix changes. When a kit is older than 6 moons, they can become an apprentice. This will change their suffix from -kit to -paw. After a cat has been an apprentice, and is deemed ready to become a warrior by their mentor and their clan's leader they can become a warrior. Warrior's can have a variety of sufixes. Usually their sufix matches a trait, whether physical or not. Or, their suffix can be the prefix of another cat. (Though this is not seen frequently). Cats who recieve the -star suffix are or were formerly leaders of a clan. These suffixes have their own pages, which can be found below:

    -kit
    -paw
    -star

Suffix Usage

Below is a chart containing all of the suffixes used throughout the first and most recent (7th) arc in the Warrior's series. Any suffixes that exist, but were not found in either arc have been ommited from data.

Suffix usage can tell us a lot about what kinds of names make main, or at least relevant characters. One that stands out like a sore thumb is the suffix -tail. In the first arc it has 12 cats with that suffix, with a total of over 2000 mentions. This can lead us to conclude that for the first arc, if a character has the suffix of -tail that they have a good chance of being a main character. However, when we look at The Broken Code, the oposite seems to be true. There are now more cats with the -tail suffix (12), however they only have 377 mentions. This can lead us to conclude that cats with the tail suffix in later books are less likely to be main characters.

Another example being the suffix -frost. There are only 3 cats with that suffix, however they have over 2500 mentions. It could be concluded that one or all of the characters with the -frost suffix are main characters. The same can be concluded with -sight (however there are only 2 cats in that situation).
